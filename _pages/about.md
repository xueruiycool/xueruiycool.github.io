Welcome! I am a Postdoctoral Associate in Community Food Systems at the [University of Florida (IFAS)](https://ifas.ufl.edu/), where I work with Dr. Catherine Campbell. I received my Ph.D. in Consumer Sciences (Economics Track) from [The Ohio State University](https://www.osu.edu/).

My research examines the economic and public-health dimensions of the food system, and spans two connected areas. The first is food safety economics: estimating the illness burden, healthcare costs, and market impacts of foodborne disease and outbreaks. The second is household food waste and sustainable food systems: how purchasing, storage, and consumption decisions shape waste, and how behavioral interventions can improve resource use. Across these projects I work with outbreak surveillance data, electronic health records, retail scanner and consumer panel data, national surveys, and citizen-science panels, combining causal inference and Bayesian modeling.

I am on the academic job market for 2026–27.
