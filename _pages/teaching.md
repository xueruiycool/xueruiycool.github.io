## Teaching Interests

I teach applied economics with an emphasis on decision-making under risk, resource allocation, and environmental sustainability, and I enjoy making quantitative and econometric methods approachable for students who do not arrive with a strong background in statistics or economics.

## Extension & Outreach

**Curriculum Developer — Growing Urban Agriculture Resilience and Development (GUARD)**<br>
University of Florida / IFAS · USDA SARE Professional Development Program · 2026–Present

I developed a 13-module online course on Florida agricultural law for urban and small-scale farmers, delivered through Canvas. Each module pairs a narrated slide deck with an ADA-accessible transcript, an ungraded knowledge check, curated resources, and a practical action checklist. The course is organized into four topic areas:

- **Setting Up Your Farm Business** — 4 modules (business structure, planning, taxes & insurance)
- **Land, Zoning & Legal Protections** — 5 modules (urban ag rules, zoning, leasing, right to farm & greenbelt)
- **Selling Your Products** — 3 modules (market channels, value-added products & agritourism)
- **Managing People & Operations** — 1 module (employment law basics for farm operators)

The materials support a statewide train-the-trainer deployment reaching Florida Cooperative Extension educators across all 67 counties.
